ଅନ୍ନ ବ୍ରହ୍ମ — Anna Brahma

Food is not just sustenance. In Odisha, food is God.

✦

"ଅନ୍ନଂ ନ ନିନ୍ଦ୍ୟାତ, ତଦ୍ ବ୍ରତମ — ପ୍ରାଣୋ ବ ଅନ୍ନମ"
"Do not dishonour food — that is the vow. Food is life itself, food is Prana."

— Taittiriya Upanishad 3.7

The kitchen of Lord Jagannath at Puri — the Rosha Ghara — is one of the largest in the world. It has 32 rooms, 240 hearths, and the fire has never been put out. Every single day, nearly 1000 cooks prepare food for up to 1 lakh devotees. New earthen pots are used each day. No modern equipment — no mixers, no gas stoves, no electric grinders. The way food is prepared today is the same way it was prepared 500 years ago.

This website is a small tribute to that ancient kitchen. The recipes collected here are drawn from old Odia books, traditional sources, and the cultural memory of Odisha — dishes that have been prepared for festivals at the Jagannath temple in Puri and in Odia households for generations.

Each dish is part of the Chhappan Bhog — the 56 offerings made daily to Lord Jagannath, Lord Balabhadra, and Maa Subhadra. The food is first offered to the deities, then to Goddess Bimala inside the Sri Mandir, after which it becomes Mahaprasad — the greatest of all prasad in all of India.

✦

The Seven Recipes

The seven dishes on this site represent different parts of the Chhappan Bhog — from the morning Sakala Dhupa to dry sweets sold at Ananda Bazar to festival pithas made in homes across Odisha.

  • — Mahaprasad Khichdi — the sacred rice and dal offering of the morning dhupa
  • — Rasabali — GI-tagged sweet from Kendrapara's Baladevjew Temple
  • — Khaja — the Lord's favourite, sold at Ananda Bazar since the 12th century
  • — Pakhala — fermented rice, offered to Jagannath since the 11th century
  • — Dalma — the lentil-vegetable stew of temples and homes, ancient as Kalinga
  • — Enduri Pitha — turmeric leaf steamed rice cake, made for Prathamastami
  • — Odia Rasagulla — mentioned in the 15th-century Jagamohana Ramayana
